About

During the early first millennium CE the island of Socotra, off the coasts of Yemen and Somalia, was a hubb of traders from all corners of the Indian Ocean. Inside the Hoq cave, a natural cave one the island, archaeologists discovered hundreds of rock inscriptions by India, South-Arabian, Bactrian, and Greek merchants. The Periplus Maris Erythraei describes the island as Dioscurides Island, a multiethnic port of trade.

Aim of the site
This website aims to gather all the primary sources on the island in a centralized digital space. From the passage in the Periplus, to a detailed rendition of the original text, translation, and commentary of each inscription, along with images, this website will create an easy platform for studying this unique island.
